Tigers

Tigers or Panthera Tigris are the largest big cats native to Asia. They have been around sense the early Pleistocene period and have few predators other than humans. Spaning across all of Asia there were 9 subspecies of tiger in total. Out of thoes 9 subspecies only 6 are not extinct.

Predators

  • Dholes
  • Elephants
  • Crocodiles
  • Bears

Subspecies

There are 9 subsoecies of tigers still alive today. Siberian tigers live in North asia and can weigh up to 475lbs. Bengal Tigers live in what is known as the Indian subcontainent which is India, Nepal, Bhutan, and Bangladesh. They can weigh up to 569lbs. The Indochinese Tiger lives in Southeast asia and can get up to 430lbs. The Malayan tiger lives in southeast asia aswell and can get up to 308lbs. South china tigers live in central and eastern China dispite their name. They can get up to 386lbs and can possibly get a blue mutation although one has never been seen. The last subspecies of living tiger is known as Sumatran tiger, living in Indonesia and getting up to 310lbs.
